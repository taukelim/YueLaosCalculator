    What is the Love Calculator based on?

    This love calculator is based on the Drake Equation and Optimal Stopping Theory.

    How does it work?

    Yue Lao's Calculator accepts two user inputs. Personal metrics such as height, age, education and income-- and dating history. The Drake Equation is applied to filter a starting population down to a dating pool. Then Optimal Stopping Theory is applied to calculate the presence of the best candidate for a relationship.

    How is my attractiveness calculated?

    Attractiveness is calculated using a linear regression and areas of bell curves based on this informal survey of mostly South East Asian women conducted in 2022, carried out by the developer of the calculator.

    For height and education, preferences of minimum and maximum male height and education are mapped onto a bell curve. For example, a male that is too tall or too short will have a smaller area range of this bell curve, while males in the ideal range of height will have the largest area range. A bell curve is used because there are less extremely short and extremely tall women compared to average height women, as with education attainment.

    For income, linear regression is used to predict the correlation between increase in income and number of women specifying that income as acceptable for dating.

    For age, a double filter is used. Based on this United Nation's data, there is a rounded average of 1% of a population within each given age. This 1% is multiplied by the input age's acceptable dating range. Next the calculator filters how many people are married within their age range, to show the final percentage.

    How is my dating pool calculated?

    Based on your attractiveness, a starting population is slowly filtered down to get your dating pool. For example, a 160cm tall guy will have women 153cm and taller women filtered off from his dating pool as they prefer males atleast 8cm taller according to the survey.

    What is the Drake Equation?

    From wikipedia:
    The Drake equation is a probabilistic argument used to estimate the number of active, communicative extraterrestrial civilizations in the Milky Way Galaxy. The equation was formulated in 1961 by Frank Drake.

    This calculator uses it to filter the number of datable people for a certain individual.

    How accurate is the calculator?

    It is not accurate as it is a representation of reality. Women's heights and education don't fit perfectly on a bell curve. A male's face and personality are not taken into account. Some women prioritize wealth over height, while this calculator treats all metrics as equal.
